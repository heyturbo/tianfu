Yelp Database Analysis: Understanding User Behavior and Business Patterns

This comprehensive analysis project explores the Yelp database to uncover patterns in user behavior, restaurant performance, and platform growth. The study examines six key tables from the Yelp database (business information, business hours, check-ins, reviews, tips, and user information), covering data from 2004 to 2017.

Project Scope: Analyzed over 5.2 million reviews, 1.3 million users, and 174,000 businesses to derive actionable insights for users, merchants, and the platform itself.

Project Objectives

This analysis was structured to provide insights from three key perspectives:

  • Customer Perspective: Understanding user review patterns, elite user behavior, and rating distributions
  • Restaurant Perspective: Analyzing factors that influence restaurant ratings, geographic trends, and operating patterns
  • Platform Perspective: Examining user growth, retention rates, and engagement patterns over time
[User Growth Chart 2004-2017 - Visualization showing the rise and eventual decline of new user registrations]

Data Methodology and Preparation

The analysis utilized a structured SQL-based approach to clean, analyze, and visualize the Yelp dataset. Before beginning the analysis, I performed extensive data cleaning to ensure quality results.

Data Sources

The dataset included six primary tables from the Yelp database:

Table Name Description Records
yelp_business Basic business information 174,567
yelp_business_hours Business operating hours 174,567
yelp_checkin Check-in information 146,350
yelp_review Review information 5,261,668
yelp_tip Tip information 1,098,324
yelp_user User information 1,326,100

Data Preparation Process

The cleaning process involved several key steps:

  1. Filtering outliers and invalid values from each table
  2. Separating currently open and closed businesses
  3. Removing businesses without operating information
  4. Eliminating records with missing critical data
  5. Creating derived tables for specific analyses

Example SQL Query

-- Calculate the count of reviews with 'useful' values greater than 1000,
-- the average star rating rounded to one decimal place,
-- and the average length of reviews rounded to the nearest whole number.
SELECT
 COUNT(sub.text) as review_count,
 ROUND(AVG(sub.stars), 1) as avg_stars,
 ROUND(AVG(sub.len), 0) as avg_length
FROM (
 -- Subquery to select relevant data from yelp_review
 SELECT stars, text, LENGTH(text) as len, useful
 FROM yelp_review
 WHERE useful > 1000
) as sub;

Key Findings and Insights

1. Customer Perspective Analysis

Elite vs. Regular Users: Elite users post approximately 10.2 times more reviews than regular users and receive 3.5-7.8 times more "useful", "funny", and "cool" votes on their reviews.

[Elite vs Regular User Engagement Metrics - Visualization showing comparison of review frequency and feedback metrics]

Analysis revealed interesting patterns about user engagement and review quality:

  • Reviews with more "useful" votes tended to be longer (averaging 1,400-2,200 characters) and gave lower ratings to restaurants (averaging 1.3-2.4 stars)
  • Negative reviews were more likely to be voted as "useful" compared to positive reviews
  • The length and star rating of a review had little correlation with whether it was perceived as "funny" or "cool"
  • Elite users received approximately 22 followers each, compared to less than 1 follower for non-elite users

2. Restaurant Perspective Analysis

Geographical and operational patterns showed clear influence on restaurant success:

[Restaurant Distribution by State - Map visualization showing concentration in Arizona and Nevada]
  • Arizona (29.91%) and Nevada (18.95%) account for nearly half of all restaurants in the dataset
  • Restaurants with higher star ratings tended to operate more days per week, with 5-star restaurants most commonly operating 5-7 days
  • Closed restaurants had operated fewer days per week (average 3.9 days) and had slightly lower ratings (average 3.51 stars) compared to all restaurants
  • 4.0-star restaurants received the most reviews, despite 5-star restaurants being common (15.78% of all restaurants)

3. Platform Perspective Analysis

User Growth Trends: After peaking in 2014 with 198,976 new users, Yelp saw declining new user registrations, dropping to 77,174 by 2017.

Platform usage patterns revealed both strengths and challenges:

  • Despite declining new user registrations, review volume continued to increase steadily through 2017
  • Elite user selection began in 2005 and stabilized at around 4,000 new elite users per year from 2008 onward
  • Annual user retention rate remained consistent at approximately 30%
  • Elite users had a dramatically higher retention rate of around 80%
  • Summer months showed peak user registration and activity

Conclusions and Recommendations

For Users

To maximize engagement and potentially achieve elite status:

  • Focus on writing informative, detailed reviews rather than stylistic ones
  • Post reviews consistently rather than sporadically
  • Add photos to reviews, as elite users post significantly more photos
  • Consider geographical factors, as users in Nevada and Arizona may have higher visibility

For Restaurant Owners

To improve ratings and visibility:

  • Maintain consistent operating hours, preferably 5+ days per week
  • Focus particularly on minimizing negative reviews, as they significantly impact overall rating
  • Consider geographical advantage when opening in states like Arizona and Nevada
  • Encourage reviews from engaged users who write thoughtful content

For Platform Development

To counter declining new user registrations:

  • Focus marketing efforts on summer months when user engagement naturally peaks
  • Consider expanding to more states or international markets
  • Enhance benefits for elite users to drive aspiration among regular users
  • Create incentives for regular users to increase retention above the current 30% rate

Technical Implementation Details

This project demonstrates several key technical skills in data analysis:

SQL Query Optimization

The analysis required sophisticated SQL queries to process millions of records efficiently. Complex joins, subqueries, and aggregations were optimized for performance.

-- Calculate the average of 'avg_review_day' values for elite users.
SELECT AVG(sub.avg_review_day) as avg_review_day_elite
FROM (
  -- Subquery to calculate 'avg_review_day' for elite users
  SELECT
    user_id,
    DATEDIFF("2017-12-11", yelping_since) as register_time,
    (review_count / DATEDIFF("2017-12-11", yelping_since)) as avg_review_day
  FROM `yelp_user`
  WHERE elite <> "None"
) as sub;
